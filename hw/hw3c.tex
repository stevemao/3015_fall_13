In class we discussed the following code.

\begin{program*}
\> plus (x,y) = x + y \\
\> plusc x y = x + y \\
\> curry f x y  =  f(x,y) \\
\> uncurry f (x,y) = f x y \\
\> (f . g) x = f (g x) \\
\> id x = x \\
\end{program*}

Recall the extensionality rule for proving functions $f,g:A\rightarrow{}B$ are equal.
\[ f= g \definedAsM \forall{}x:A. f x = g x \]

As was shown in class, prove the following using extensionality.

\[\begin{array}{ll}
% i.) & uncurry \; plusc = plus \\
i.) & curry \; plus = plusc \\
ii.) & curry (uncurry \; plusc) = plusc \\
iii.) & uncurry (curry \; plus) = plus \\
iv.) & (f . id) = f \\
v.) & (id . f) = f 

\end{array}\]
